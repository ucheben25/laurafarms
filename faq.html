Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about investing with Laura Farms — from getting started to receiving your returns.

🌱 Getting Started

It's simple. Browse our three investment packages (Seedling, Harvest, Estate) on the Packages page. Click "Request a Quote" on your preferred plan to send us your investment details. Our team will respond within 24 hours with a personalized investment proposal and next steps.
Our minimum investment amounts are: Seedling: $5,000 | Harvest: $25,000 | Estate: $100,000. All investments are per 12-month cycle. There is no maximum investment limit for any package.
Yes. Laura Farms is open to all US residents 18 years and older. We are based in Omaha, Nebraska and currently serve investors across all 50 states. International investors may contact us to discuss eligibility.
For the Seedling and Harvest packages, accreditation is not required. The Estate package ($100,000+) may require accredited investor status per applicable securities regulations. Our team will walk you through any requirements during the onboarding process.

📈 Investment & Returns

Based on historical farm performance: Seedling — 12 to 15% per year, Harvest — 18 to 22% per year, Estate — 25 to 32% per year. These are projections based on 12 years of operations and are not guaranteed. Agricultural returns depend on crop yields, commodity prices, and weather conditions.
Returns are distributed at the end of each 12-month harvest cycle via ACH bank transfer or wire transfer directly to your registered bank account. Estate investors receive priority payout processing, typically within 5 business days of cycle close. Harvest investors receive payouts within 10 business days.
Absolutely. We offer a Reinvestment Option that automatically applies your returns to start a new investment cycle, allowing for compounded growth. Harvest and Estate investors also get access to compound interest calculators in the investor portal.
Yes. Returns from your Laura Farms investment are considered ordinary income and are subject to federal and state income taxes. We issue a 1099 form to all investors at the end of each tax year. We recommend consulting a qualified tax advisor regarding your specific situation.

🛡️ Risk & Security

We carry comprehensive USDA-backed crop and livestock insurance on all our farms. In the event of a natural disaster or significant crop failure, insurance proceeds protect the principal investment. All packages include some level of crop insurance, with Estate having the most comprehensive coverage. Laura Farms has maintained positive investor returns throughout its 12-year history, including through drought years.
Your investment is backed by real, tangible farm assets — land, equipment, and crop inventory — which have intrinsic value. While your principal is not guaranteed in the same way a bank CD would be, the asset-backed nature of agricultural investment provides a strong layer of security that purely financial products do not.
Laura Farms LLC operates in compliance with applicable federal and state securities regulations. Our investment structures are reviewed by qualified legal counsel. We follow USDA standards for all farming operations. Please review our Investment Disclosures before investing.
Investment commitments are for the full 12-month cycle. Early withdrawal is generally not available, as funds are deployed into farm operations at the start of the cycle. In exceptional circumstances, you may contact your account manager to discuss options. We strongly recommend only investing funds you can commit for the full cycle period.

🌾 Farm Operations

We manage over 50,000 acres of farmland across 7 US states including Nebraska, Iowa, Kansas, Illinois, Missouri, South Dakota, and Minnesota. Our farms produce row crops (corn, soybeans, wheat), livestock (cattle, hogs), and specialty crops (sunflowers, alfalfa).
Estate package investors receive an exclusive annual farm visit and investor retreat, typically held in September at our flagship Nebraska farm. For other investors, we occasionally host open farm days — announcements are made through the investor portal. General site tours may also be arranged by contacting our team.
We hold USDA-certified sustainable agriculture designations. Our practices include no-till and cover cropping to preserve soil health, precision irrigation to reduce water usage, integrated pest management to minimize chemical use, and carbon sequestration programs. Sustainability isn't just ethics for us — it protects long-term farm productivity and your investment.
